Biography

Kaan Okumus received the B.Sc. degree in electrical and electronics engineering from Middle East Technical University (METU), Ankara, Turkey, in 2021, with a specialization in signal processing and telecommunications. He received the M.Sc. degree in communication systems from École Polytechnique Fédérale de Lausanne (EPFL), Lausanne, Switzerland, in 2023. His master’s thesis was conducted at the Imaging Systems Group, EPFL, under the supervision of Dr. Matthieu Simeoni, and focused on graph signal processing methods for inverse problems. During his time at EPFL, he also worked as a research assistant and contributed to the development of the open-source Python package Pyxu.

Since 2023, he has been pursuing the Ph.D. degree with the Communication Systems Group, Department of Electrical Engineering, Chalmers University of Technology, Gothenburg, Sweden, under the supervision of Prof. Giuseppe Durisi and Prof. Erik G. Ström.

His research interests are in wireless communications and information theory, with a particular focus on massive random access systems. His current work emphasizes scalable and practical algorithm design for large-scale wireless systems, with applications to distributed MIMO architectures, federated learning, and multi-target localization. He is a graduate student member of the IEEE.
